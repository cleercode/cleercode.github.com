Shelf

Web App

What do possessions say about a person?

That's the question we explored with Shelf, a social network based around the products that people love. The goals of Shelf were as follows:

  • Build communities based on product interests: Products offer a common ground for people who would otherwise be strangers to interact on a level that is engaging and yet not intrusive.
  • Supply relevant information about products that extends beyond technical specifications: For shoppers, some of the most useful information about potential purchases is based on personal experiences, not technical details.
  • Keep track and showcase product progression: Walking through someone's bedroom and seeing their favorite things tells a lot about a person, and this experience can be mirrored in digital form.

We designed Shelf to work as an engaging community by targeting two main user groups:

  • Enthusiasts: Product lovers wanting to discuss new releases and how they use their products
  • Smart shoppers: People seeking information about potential purchases

Process

The process involved multiple ideation sessions, user interviews and affinity diagramming, paper wireframing, digital wireframing, and development of a prototype. Though I was involved with each stage, my primary contribution was the final prototype, built in HTML and CSS.
