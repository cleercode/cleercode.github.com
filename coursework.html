Coursework

As a computer science student at Carnegie Mellon, my coursework ranges from discrete mathematics and computing theory to various levels of programming.

My interests in computer science include machine learning and high-level programming algorithms.

Fundamental Data Structures and Algorithms

Teaches advanced data structures and algorithms in Java.

General theory covered includes how to select data structures and algorithms, how to verify and debug programs, and how to analyze algorithms. Specific topics include dynamic programming, sorting, searching, data compression, and trees and graphs.

Near the end of the course, the entire class split into 50+ groups of two and wrote automated chess players. Out of all the teams, my partner and I ranked second.

Assignments

  • Chess bot
  • File compressor
  • Six Degrees of Kevin Bacon solver

Introduction to Computer Systems

An intensive machine-level programming course in assembly language and C.

Topics covered include memory organization, process control, and networking technology.

Assignments

  • Memory allocator
  • Cache simulator
  • Buffer overflow attack
  • System process puzzles

Great Theoretical Ideas of Computer Science

An intensive computer science theory and discrete math course.

Teaches general skills such as mathematical proofs, teamwork, and problem-solving under stress. Also gives a broad but effective overview of topics like combinatorial games, probability, cryptography, algebraic theory, graphs, and automata.
